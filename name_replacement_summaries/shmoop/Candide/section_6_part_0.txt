{"name": "Chapter 7", "url": "https://web.archive.org/web/20201024064035/https://www.shmoop.com/study-guides/literature/candide/summary/chapter-7", "summary": "The Old Woman offers Candide a meal, ointment for his wounds, and a good night's sleep. For several days the woman tends to Candide, but refuses to provide any explanation for her kindness. Once he stops bleeding profusely, the woman leads Candide a few miles into the country to yet another strange house. She brings a veiled woman into the room; Candide lifts the veil and finds that the woman is Cunegonde. Cunegonde survived the attack on her family, but was raped and disemboweled. The rest of her family was killed. Candide recounts all of his experiences since their last meeting.", "analysis": ""}
